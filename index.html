Who are the haenyeo?

Korea's last sea women

For centuries, the haenyeo of Jeju have dived with no oxygen — holding their breath metres down, gathering abalone and urchin by hand, many still working into their 70s and 80s. Theirs is a rare, all-women culture, recognised by UNESCO in 2016. But their numbers have fallen from some 30,000 to roughly 2,400 today — and 63% are now over 70. Meet them while you still can.

I'm Soraya — founder and your guide.

While working as an English guide on Jeju, I often found myself beside the haenyeo, translating their words. Their lives moved me — and it saddened me to watch something so inspiring quietly disappear. I wanted to become the kind of fierce, graceful woman these grandmothers are, so I enrolled in haenyeo school myself.

Now I lead the journey I love most — the one that brings you to the haenyeo themselves. Jeju is a deeply spiritual island; through yoga and meditation here, I found a healing I never expected. That is what I most want to give you: to leave with the island's quiet held inside you, and a little of the haenyeo's strength.

I also share the haenyeo's stories through the Vamos a Jeju podcast and journal.

Questions, answered

What language are the retreats in?
English throughout — guided personally by Soraya, who lives on Jeju and translates for the haenyeo. Prefer Spanish? We run Spanish-language journeys separately — just reach out and ask.
How does booking work — do I pay now?
We're taking inquiries only for now. There's no payment at this stage; after a short conversation with Soraya, your spot is confirmed. Online booking and payment will open later in 2026.
How do I get there, and where do we meet?
Fly into Jeju International Airport (CJU). When you book, we send a full guidebook with the exact meeting point, how to get there, and everything you need to know.
What should I pack?
No guesswork — once you book, your personal guidebook covers exactly what to bring and what to expect, day by day.
Where will I sleep?
Quiet Tide is a day experience — there's no overnight, so you keep your own hotel. Still Waters includes one traditional templestay night — simple bedding on a warm ondol (heated) floor, monastic and intentional — and the remaining nights in a comfortable coastal hotel with beds. Deep Roots follows the same spirit: one temple night, the rest in comfort.
How fit do I need to be? Is it difficult?
Gentle to moderate. Expect easy forest walks, calm time by the shore, and unhurried days — you always set your own pace, and nothing is mandatory. Please share any health, mobility, or medical needs in your application so we can prepare and adapt.
Do I have to freedive?
There's no deep diving. With Still Waters & Deep Roots you go into the water alongside the haenyeo — looking, floating at the surface, and trying a little gathering with them. It's about being with the sea women, not descending deep. And if you're not at home in the water, a gentle shore walk is just as welcome. Quiet Tide has no diving at all.
What if I have accessibility or mobility needs?
We want to be honest so your time with us is wonderful: much of the retreat unfolds on Jeju's natural terrain — uneven village shores, rocky coastline, temple steps, and time by the water — so it suits travellers who are comfortable on their feet over varied ground. If you have any mobility or medical considerations, please share them in your application and we'll talk it through openly to find the right fit for you.
What about meals and dietary needs?
Temple cuisine is fully vegan — no meat and no oshinchae (the five pungent vegetables, such as garlic and onion). Tell us about any allergies or other needs and we'll do our best to accommodate.
Can I come on my own?
Quiet Tide is a private day for 2–4 guests (a couple or small group), at one flat price. Travelling solo? Our multi-day retreats — Still Waters and Deep Roots — warmly welcome solo guests. Larger group (5+)? Just send an inquiry.
Do I need travel insurance?
We strongly recommend comprehensive travel insurance covering trip cancellation, medical care, and any in-water activities. Insurance is not included in the retreat price.
What is your cancellation & refund policy?
A deposit secures your place and is non-refundable. For the remaining balance: cancel 60+ days before the start date for a full refund; 30–59 days, 50% refunded; within 30 days, non-refundable. If we ever have to cancel a retreat, you'll receive a full refund. We strongly recommend travel insurance. (We're taking inquiries only for now — no payment is taken yet, so this policy applies once booking opens.)
